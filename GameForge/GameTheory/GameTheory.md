## The Basics of fun ##

So what exactly makes a game fun? This is sort of a hard thing to answer as its entirly subjective. The answer really is in the eye of the player....However.... I want to narrow the scope of this question from looking at things from that partuculare perspective. 

What if its actually not as subjective as you might initially think? 

we will expand on these more in later sections but here is my general list of things to consider when making a game.

- What is my core gamplay loop, and , dose it feel good? 
- Do I have enough systems? (Crafting, puzzles, combat, exploration, economy, etc)
- Do my systems have enough depth? 
- Do players play the game, or , dose the game play itself? 
- If my game features exolporation, how dose that feel? 
- Is my game bloated with points of interest (that arent interesting)?
- Do I have item bloat? 
- Do I have quest bloat? 
- Are my dungeons boring? (irespective of graphics)
- etc....

## Core gameplay loop ## 
When we are making a game the very first thing we need to establish is the core gameplay loop. 

Translated this means. The thing the player will be doing repeatidly for the rest of the game. 

Lets look at a real world example, we will use a game most players would be familliar with to start. 

What is the core gameplay loop of The Legend of Zelda series (excluding BOTW - TOTK)? 

Lets break it down
1. Run to new area
2. find the dungeon,
3. solve the puzzles
4. get new item
5. beat boss
6. go to new area
7. repeat.

but wait their is more that happens in between these moments right? Im going to call the in-betweens sub loops. So really our list should look more like...

OK that is a much bigger list and maybe we can debate the specifics, but I think that their is one thing we can all agree on. 

IF THE SUB LOOPS ARE BORING THE CORE LOOP WILL BE BORING!!!!

## What the heck is a sub loop ##
My definition of a sub loop is simply this...

What is the player doing in the small moments of the game? Or rather, what do I the developer want them to be doing inbetween my core loop. 

For example of Im making an exploration game, my player probably has to physically run from point A -> B. What are they going to do while that happens? (Fight mobs, get money, maybe some crafting... oh look, a squirrle....) 

Thats a sub loop. Simply put, the small things make the larger things worth it. 

- Its not fun to fight a boss, the same way everytime
- Its not fun to craft a new weapon that looks just like the weapon I already have (Unless youre a min max'r....again subjective)
- its not fun to walk from A -> B if the player cant take some side roads.
